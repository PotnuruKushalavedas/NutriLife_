⚖️ Dietary Harmony

Importance of a Balanced Diet

A balanced diet combines all essential food groups in the right proportions — fueling the body, protecting against disease, and supporting mental well-being throughout life.

The Healthy Plate Model

🥬 Vegetables & Fruits
50%
🌾 Whole Grains
25%
🍗 Protein Foods
25%

Fill half your plate with colorful vegetables and fruits, one quarter with whole grains, and one quarter with lean protein sources.

❤️

Heart Health

Reduces cholesterol, blood pressure, and cardiovascular disease risk through fiber-rich and low-sodium food choices.

🧠

Brain Function

Omega-3 fatty acids, B-vitamins, and iron support memory, concentration, and cognitive development in all age groups.

🛡️

Strong Immunity

Vitamins A, C, D, zinc, and protein strengthen the immune system and accelerate recovery from illness.

🦴

Bone & Muscle Strength

Calcium, vitamin D, and adequate protein maintain skeletal density and prevent muscle wasting in aging populations.

💡 Practical Guidance

Healthy Eating Tips

Simple, actionable habits that families and communities can adopt today to improve daily nutrition without complex meal planning.

01

Eat the Rainbow

Include fruits and vegetables of different colors daily to ensure a broad spectrum of vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants.

02

Choose Whole Grains

Replace refined flour with brown rice, oats, millets, and whole wheat for sustained energy and better digestive health.

03

Limit Processed Foods

Reduce intake of packaged snacks, sugary drinks, and fried foods that are high in salt, sugar, and unhealthy fats.

04

Pair Iron with Vitamin C

Combine iron-rich foods like spinach or lentils with citrus fruits to significantly improve iron absorption in the body.

05

Stay Hydrated

Drink adequate water throughout the day. Limit sugary beverages and excessive caffeine, especially for children and elderly.

06

Practice Portion Control

Use smaller plates, eat slowly, and listen to hunger cues to maintain healthy body weight and prevent overeating.
